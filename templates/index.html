SkyNote

Improving the practice of musical expression

About the experiment

SkyNote is an intelligent learning assistant for musicians developed at Universitat Pompeu Fabra as part of project TELMI. It has been designed to use innovative detection technologies that analyze the movement and the sound of the performing artist in real time, providing them with tools to detect and correct errors, build awareness of their technique, and practice more efficiently.

Now, we are interested in taking this concept one step further by exploring whether technology can help musicians work on their expressive performance skills.

During the months of June and July, at the UPF Poblenou campus in Barcelona, we will be working with violin players to determine how effective SkyNote can be as an assistant to the practice of musical expression.

If you are 18 years old or older and can play the violin, we would like to invite you to participate in this experiment. To do so, please provide your contact information below and we will get in touch. Thank you for being interested in shaping the future of musical education!


What will happen if I take part?

As a participant in this experiment you will be asked to come to UPF on a day of your choosing and perform some exercises for musical expression practice. The diagram below outlines the basic structure of the experiment. Based on the participants' feedback as well as measurements taken from the recorded audio we will form an assessment about the impact SkyNote has on expressive performance practice.

A researcher from our group will accompany you at all times, give specific instructions and clear any doubts you may have in the process.

Questions

How long does the experiment take?

Previous Next

The full experiment should take approximately 1 hour.

Are there risks/benefits in taking part?

Previous Next

There are no foreseeable risks in taking part. As part of cautionary measures to prevent COVID-19, all equipment will be sanitized prior to the arrival of each participant, all personnel involved will be required to wear masks at all times and the room utilized for the experiment will be left vacant between rounds for maximum air renovation. Unfortunately, at the moment we are unable to reward your participation, but we hope to make it as amusing and instructional as possible.

Will my taking part in this project be kept confidential?

Previous Next

Yes. All recordings as well as collected written data will be stored anonymously. While audio recordings without identifiable information may be shown to the public or used by other violinists in the experiment, we will never show recordings that could be used to identify you without your express permission.

What will happen to the results of the research project?

Previous Next

The data will be presented at national and international conferences as well as published in academic journals. All information about participants will remain anonymous. Collected data may be used for additional or subsequent research. If you are interested to learn more about the research results, you can always contact the research team.
