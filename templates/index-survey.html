Withdraw {% else %} Welcome! The experiment you are invited to participate is part of project TELMI, which focuses on developing new technologies for learning musical instruments. In this survey, we are interested in studying the impact of variations in dynamics (loudness) and timing in the perception of musical expression. Our aim is to evaluate how our computer models fare in terms of their likeness to human performance.

In the following pages you will be asked to listen to computer generated audio samples and compare them according to your perception of how well they approximate a human rendition. The survey should take approximately 30 minutes to complete. Below you can find more details regarding our data usage policy. For any questions, feel free to contact the researchers.

Which are the listening requirements?
You will need a standard laptop/desktop computer with internet connection. For optimal audio quality we recommend Chrome or Mozilla Firefox browsers. Internet Explorer and Safari are not supported. We encourage you to perform the test in a quiet room using high quality headphones.

Note on Participation and Data Usage
Participation in this survey is voluntary and open to anyone aged 16 or older. All responses will be anonymised, and the data collected will be presented at national and international conferences as well as published in academic journals, and may be used for subsequent research. If you decide to take part, by beginning this survey you are providing your informed consent. You will still be free to withdraw at any time. If you are interested to learn more about the results or if you would like your data removed from the project please contact the researchers.
